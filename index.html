Co-Living, Care, and Senior Living 2026: Where London Lender Appetite Is Quietly Concentrating

Beyond the PBSA and BTR headlines, three operationally-led residential asset classes are quietly winning the lender-appetite competition in Greater London. Co-living. Care homes. Senior living. Each has its own dynamics, and each is delivering capital structures that make 2026 schemes financeable where pure open-market resi does not.

Co-living

Co-living forward funding clears 5.75 to 6.25% net yields in London. Operator activity is concentrated in Stratford, Walthamstow, and the connected outer ring where transport access supports the renter demographic. The schemes typically run 200 to 600 units in 8- to 14-storey new-build form.

Senior development finance prices around 6.5% per annum at 65% LTGDV. The forward-funding visibility tightens the construction-phase mezzanine layer materially.

Care homes

Care home development clears 6.0 to 6.75% net yields. Demand depth is structurally rising as the population ages and CCG/local-authority placement pressures sustain occupancy. London-specific demand is strongest in Bromley, Croydon, and the connected outer-south corridor.

Senior debt at 6.5 to 7.0% per annum at 60 to 65% LTGDV — slightly tighter than resi due to operational-risk overlay. JV equity from sector specialists is active.

Senior living

Senior living, distinct from care, targets active-retirement age cohorts. Forward funding clears 5.5 to 6.0% net yields — comparable to PBSA. Demand concentrates in town-centre locations with good amenity, often Bromley, Sutton, and parts of Greater London’s outer ring.

Senior development finance at 6.5% per annum at 65% LTGDV with forward-funding visibility.

Why this matters

For schemes that don’t clear viability as open-market resi, repositioning to one of these operational asset classes is increasingly the deal-saving move. The institutional take-out at 5.5 to 6.5% net yields supports a development underwrite that pure-sale economics no longer does.
